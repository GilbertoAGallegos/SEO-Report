Organic Ranking of Search Results

Organic ranking refers to the position a webpage holds in search engine results based on its relevance and quality, independent of paid advertising. Search engines, such as Google, determine this ranking through a multi-step process: crawling, rendering, indexing, and ranking (Search Engine Land, 2024).


In Google’s case,

  • Crawling:

    • Crawling is the foundational step in search engine indexing, where automated bots navigate web pages via links and sitemaps and download their text, images, and video files so Google can access the raw content (Google, 2023). These bots, often called spiders or crawlers, continuously scan the web to discover new or updated content. Efficient crawling ensures that relevant information is available for users in real time. By accessing this raw data, search engines like Google can understand what each page offers and determine its relevance to user queries.

  • Rendering:

    • Rendering is the stage where the browser interprets the page’s source code (including HTML for structure, CSS for styling, and JavaScript for dynamic behavior) to construct a visual representation. This process ensures that what users see on their screens matches the intended design and functionality of the website. Rendering also helps assess how interactive elements and dynamic content behave, which can influence how the page is indexed. Pages that fail to render properly may be misinterpreted or excluded from search results.

  • Indexing:

    • Indexing is the process where search engines examine the content and metadata of each crawled page to understand its subject matter, structure, and context. This includes keywords, headings, alt text, and other signals that help determine how the page should be categorized. Once analyzed, the information is stored in Google’s vast index, allowing the search engine to quickly retrieve and rank pages based on relevance to user queries (Google, 2023). A well-indexed page is more likely to appear in search results, especially if it aligns with commonly searched terms and follows SEO best practices. Poorly structured content may be ignored or ranked lower.

  • Ranking:

    • Ranking is the final step in the search process, where a search engine’s algorithms evaluate indexed pages using multiple factors to decide which ones should appear first in search results. Factors such as page speed, HTTPS security, content quality, and user engagement are some of the ones used to determine which indexed pages best match a user’s query. Search engines also consider user intent, device type, and location to personalize results. The goal is to deliver the most useful and trustworthy content at the top of the results page.


The importance of organic ranking lies in its ability to provide consistent, high-quality traffic without the cost of paid promotion. Pages that rank well organically benefit from greater visibility, credibility, and user trust, which contribute to long-term usability. To maintain strong organic performance, websites must continually optimize their content, technical infrastructure, and user experience to ensure they meet evolving search engine standards and user expectations.

Analysis of Current Web Pages

An evaluation of the current website reveals several key factors that influence its performance in organic search rankings. By analyzing on-page elements, content structure, and other SEO practices, we can identify both the strengths that support visibility and the weaknesses that may suppress it.


What the Site Does Well

  • Clear Service Offerings:

    • The Carolina & Company Salon website does a great job of clearly presenting its services. Each treatment, such as balayage, keratin smoothing, or basic haircuts, are described in a way that’s easy for both users and search engines to understand. This clarity helps Google and other search engines categorize the site properly and match it to relevant search queries.

    Additionally, the use of specific service names throughout the site supports keyword targeting. When users search for terms like “Dallas balayage” or “keratin treatment near me,” the site has a better chance of appearing in results. This not only boosts visibility but also attracts users who are already interested in those exact services.

  • Local Relevance and Reference:

    • Local SEO is a major strength of the site. It prominently displays the salon’s physical address in Dallas, TX, along with a local phone number. These details help search engines verify the business’s location, which is essential for appearing in local search results and map listings.

    The site also incorporates geo-targeted phrases like “Dallas hair salon” and “salons near me,” which align with how users search for local services. This increases the chances of the site showing up when someone in the Dallas area is looking for a nearby salon. These local signals are especially important for mobile users who often search with location activated as an option.

  • Responsive Design for Various Devices:

    • The website’s responsive design ensures that it functions well across all devices, from desktops to smartphones. This is crucial because Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning it evaluates the mobile version of a site when determining rankings. A smooth mobile experience helps the site perform better in search results.

    Responsive design also improves user experience. Visitors can easily navigate the site, read service descriptions, and book appointments without struggling with layout issues. This ease of use encourages longer visits and more interactions, which are positive signals for SEO and can lead to higher conversion rates.


What Needs Improvement

  • Page Title:

    • The website uses symbols in its keyword page title that are ignored or invalid. Search engines, such as Google, often strip out or ignore symbols like “+” and certain hyphens, which means they add little to the SEO (Google Help).

    It is recommended that these symbols are replaced with clear words and recognized separators in order to improve readability and ranking potential. Replacing all “+” with the word “and” could be a potential solution. Alternatively, the ampersand symbol “&” can fulfill the intended original meaning while being an acceptable symbol.

  • Brand Name is Ambiguous:

    • The name “C + Co” may be unclear to both search engines and potential customers. Abbreviated or stylized brand names can make it harder for search engines to associate the site with relevant search queries. This can lead to missed opportunities in organic rankings and brand recognition.

    Using the full business name, “Carolina and Company Salon”, in key SEO elements such as page titles, meta descriptions, and header tags helps search engines better understand the brand and improves visibility for users who search using the complete name. Consistent use of the full name also builds trust and reinforces brand identity across search results.

Traffic Boosting Ideas

1. Create Localized Blogs & Visual Content

Develop blog posts and service guides that connect hair and nail services with the culture and lifestyle of Dallas, especially within the Bishop Arts District. These posts should include:

  • Original photos of salon work
  • Relevant service pages
  • Local references and seasonal relevance

This strategy enhances the salon’s authority in the local market, improves organic search visibility, and provides shareable content for social media platforms.

2. Launch Social Proof Campaigns

Encourage customers to share their before and after photos on Instagram and TikTok using a branded hashtag such as #CarolinaAndCoGlow. Incentives may include:

  • Free product samples
  • Entry into a monthly prize draw

This campaign builds trust through authentic client endorsements, increases visibility of service pages, and fosters a sense of community around the brand.
